On learning to code

November 7th 2021

Coding

Ahhh coding, the classical laungauge of the geeks. From sql to python and Jquery coding can seem to go on forever. As a begginer(like my self) coding can be one of the hardest skills you will come across. The common pit falls that many people who want to learn programmign face is the starting fallacy. This takes many forms, the most common ones are the classical "What language do I learn?" or the ever present "What bootcamp/curriculum should I use?". Unfortunately this is very hard to extinguish in coding pupils because their are so many different paths and languages in coding. People have heated debates over which language is better for begginers to learn. "Java" script is the devils tounge" I hear dev's say; Front-end is more intuitive. Keep reading for my solution.

My solution

If you cant read this section here is the TLDR: Pick a bootcamp and stick to it. So as unorthadox as this may seem In practice this is very affective, the starting language you choose does not really matter as long as it has a known syntax and documentation what matters is motivation to learn. People that constantly switch bootcamps dont learn or retain any information beacause when they switch bootcamps , they leave their previous knowledge with them. Another note: Do not pay for a coding bootcamp. Many people may furiously disagree with me and thats ok. But I strongly belive that learning with a freebootcamp will teach you how to seach for information more effiecently. These are the skills that real front/back end developers will accaully use. Now I will now admit something embarrasing . I PAID FOR CODECADEMY PRO. There. I said it. From my expirence with it i can tell you it is simply not worth it when compared to Free boot camps like The Odin Project are far more in depth and life-like. The Odin Project is a comprehensive guide in the world of programming and I highly reccomend it to any aspiring developers. Another great resource is FreeCodeCamp. FreeCodeCamp uses a certificate based system to fund your "motivation-bank" and push you to complete hundreds of ours of programming lessons. Whatever your choice is, just pick one and stick with it.

By Lior Benshoshan
